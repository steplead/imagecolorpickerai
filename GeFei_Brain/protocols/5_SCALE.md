## 2. The "Frying Beans" Strategy (Internal Linking) ðŸ”„
For sites with 10k+ pages, static menus are not enough. You must "stir fry" your links to get Google to crawl deep content.

### A. Dynamic Rotation
*   **Mechanism**: On high-authority pages (Home, Category), rotate the "Recommended" or "Related" links every hour/day.
*   **Goal**: Ensure the crawler eventually touches *every* deep page (the "cold beans") without flooding the homepage.

### B. "Stars Surrounding the Moon" (Multi-Pathing)
Create multiple logical paths to the same high-value page to prove importance:
1.  **Direct**: Home -> Detail
2.  **Categorical**: Home -> Category -> Detail
3.  **Attribute**: Home -> "Red Colors" -> Detail
4.  **Behavioral**: "Popular this Week" -> Detail

## 3. Search Result Transformation
Do not let raw search pages (`/search?q=keyword`) dilute your site.
*   **The Tactic**: If users constantly search for "Dark Mode", create a **Static List Page** (`/collections/dark-mode`) and map the search query to it.
*   **Benefits**:
    1.  **Persistent URL**: Ranks better than query parameters.
    2.  **Behavior Accumulation**: Aggregates all user clicks/dwell time on one stable URL, signaling "Quality" to Google.

## 4. Ecosystem Metabolism (Pruning) âœ‚ï¸
A healthy body excretes waste. A healthy site must prune dead pages.
*   **The Rule**: If a page has **0 Traffic** and **0 Impressions** for >6 Months:
    1.  **301 Redirect**: To a relevant parent category (if it has backlinks).
    2.  **410 Gone**: If it serves no purpose.
    3.  **Noindex**: Keep it for users, but hide from Google to save Crawl Budget.


## 6. The "Mindset of the Giant" (Strategic Wisdom) ðŸ§ 
Scaling a site is also a psychological game. Use these principles to avoid failure.

### A. "The Rope of the Baby Elephant" (Mental Limits)
> *Story*: A baby elephant is tied with a rope and learns it cannot escape. When fully grown, it could easily snap the rope, but it still believes it is trapped.
*   **Application**: Do not stay in "Small Tool" mode forever. Once you have skills, snap the rope. Attack bigger markets. Do not be afraid of 1M+ pages.

### B. Avoid the "Giants' Garden"
*   **The Rule**: Never compete with Adobe, Salesforce, or heavily funded VC startups.
*   **The Trap**: Do not build "Electronic Signature" or "File Storage" apps. Users require immense trust for these. You cannot win.
*   **The Path**: Stick to "Light Utility" (Calculators, Generators, Converters, Directories) where trust barriers are low.

### C. The "Certainty of Thresholds" (Barriers are Friends)
*   **Insight**: Most people avoid hard problems (e.g., "Need a local company registration in Japan").
*   **Strategy**: If there is a barrier that money or effort can solve (e.g., registering a foreign entity), **DO IT**.

### A. The Marketing Mix (The "SaaS" Playbook)
*   **SEO is Baseline**: It's the foundation, not the whole house.
*   **Layer 2 - Trust Platforms**: For B2B/SaaS, you MUST be on **G2**, **Capterra**, and **Trustpilot**.

### B. Team Composition
*   **The Trap**: "Freelancer Forever." (You eventually lose to young, hungry teams).
*   **The Shift**:
    *   **Phase 1**: Earn via Skills (Solo).
    *   **Phase 2**: Earn via Team (Management).
    *   **Phase 3**: Earn via Capital/Resources (Investment).
*   **Rule**: "If you don't evolve to Phase 2, your income hits a ceiling and shrinks."
